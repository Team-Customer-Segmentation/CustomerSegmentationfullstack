Outlier detection identifies data points that deviate significantly from the rest of the dataset, which can distort analysis results. Techniques like Z-score, IQR, and DBSCAN clustering are commonly used to detect and manage outliers, ensuring more accurate and reliable insights.

Algorithms Selection

Algorithm selection involves choosing the most suitable machine learning algorithm based on the datasetâ€™s characteristics, the problem type, and performance requirements. Methods like K-Means, DBSCAN, and Hierarchical Clustering are evaluated for tasks like customer segmentation to ensure optimal results.

  1. Hierarchical ClusteringFor creating nested clusters in a tree-like structure.

  2. K-Means ClusteringFor partitioning data into distinct groups.

  3. DBSCAN (Density-Based Spatial Clustering of Applications with Noise) For identifying clusters of varying shapes and handling noise.

  4. Gaussian Mixture Models (GMM)A probabilistic model that assumes data is generated from a mixture of Gaussian distributions, allowing for complex, overlapping clusters.

Applying Various Algorithms

Applying K-Means, GMM, DBSCAN, and Hierarchical Clustering allows for diverse approaches to customer segmentation, each handling clusters differently. K-Means and GMM form spherical and probabilistic clusters, respectively, DBSCAN excels at identifying clusters with noise, while HC builds nested clusters based on distances.

K-Means Algorithm

Applying K-Means involves using the Elbow Method and Silhouette Score to determine the optimal number of clusters (K) based on features such as 'Total_Spend' and 'Total_No_of_Items'. The Elbow Method identifies the K value where the within-cluster variance significantly decreases, while the Silhouette Score evaluates the quality of clustering by measuring how similar points are within the same cluster compared to others.

Finalizing Algorithm - Comparing the above mentioned Algorithms

In this analysis, we apply a comparison between K-Means and Hierarchical Clustering (HC) to evaluate their performance in customer segmentation. The results of this comparison, outlined below, highlight the strengths and weaknesses of each algorithm, helping us determine the most suitable approach for our dataset and objectives.

Applying Comparision between the applied Algorithms

The results of the silhouette score comparison indicate that K-Means outperforms the other algorithms, achieving a silhouette score of '0.3980'. This suggests that K-Means provides better-defined clusters compared to Hierarchical Clustering, which has a score of '0.3622', indicating moderate clustering quality. In contrast, Gaussian Mixture Models yield a lower silhouette score of '0.1681', signifying less effective clustering. Overall, these results suggest that K-Means is the most suitable algorithm for customer segmentation in this analysis.
